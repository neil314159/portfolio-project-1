Where should you work remotely in Ireland?

The nature of work in Ireland has changed. In the last few years we have seen the resilient and adaptable way in which people have done their jobs in difficult circumstances. In the aftermath of the pandemic, many are keen to take advantage of new technologies, workflows and attitude to work. Increasingly, companies are starting to realise that how and where their employees work is not as important as before.

This has lead to an explosion in the numbers of poole who are taking a fresh look at their job and the new possibilities in how and where they can live and work. This shift to remote work represents a fundamental change which is unlikely to go away anytime soon.

Here you will find a guide to the best spots in Ireland to live and work remotely, as well as some recommended equipment and a newsletter to keep you up to date on the latest developments in working practices.

These are the criteria we used

We have conducted in-depth and detailed research on all the towns and cities in Ireland, and compiled a list of suitable locations. We then compared and contrasted each location using our database to see which potential locations would tick all the boxes for anyone looking to work remotely. We examined the following criteria to decide on how to rank our results.

  1. House price - the average price for a home in each town
  2. Rental Price - the price you can expect to pay to rent a house in each area
  3. Crime rate - the number of crimes per 1000people
  4. Coffe shops - the number of coffee shops per 1000 people to make sure you get your caffeine fix
